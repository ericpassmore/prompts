# Regulatory Capability Implications

## Conclusion

No special regulatory capability requirements are imposed by the current scope beyond normal repository governance and access control.

## Implications For The Idea

- The process can optimize for low-friction incident capture without introducing domain-specific compliance checkpoints.
- Validation can focus on engineering quality signals such as rework reduction, faster issue triage, and lower repeated-friction rates.
- Access and provenance still matter because incidents originate in multiple repositories, but those are governance concerns rather than a domain regulatory program.

## Revalidation Triggers

- Addition of external-user data capture beyond ordinary repository artifacts.
- Automated enforcement decisions that affect users outside repository workflow governance.
- Integration with regulated business processes rather than internal skill maintenance.
